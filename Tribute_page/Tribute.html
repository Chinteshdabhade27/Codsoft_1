Swami Vivekananda

12 January 1863 - 4 July 1902

Arise, awake, and stop not till the goal is reached.

Biography In Short


Swami Vivekananda, born Narendranath Datta on January 12, 1863, in Calcutta (now Kolkata), was a prominent Indian Hindu monk and philosopher

 

Early Life and Education

Born into an aristocratic Bengali family, Vivekananda exhibited a keen interest in spirituality from a young age. He pursued higher education at the University of Calcutta, earning a Bachelor of Arts degree.

 

Spiritual Journey

In 1881, Vivekananda met his spiritual mentor, Ramakrishna, and became his devoted disciple. After Ramakrishna's passing, Vivekananda traveled extensively across India as a wandering monk, gaining deep insights into the country's socio-economic conditions.

 

Parliament of the World's Religions, 1893

Vivekananda gained international recognition when he represented Hinduism at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago in 1893. His opening words, "Sisters and brothers of America," received a standing ovation, and his eloquent speeches introduced Vedanta and Yoga to the Western audience.

 

Establishment of Ramakrishna Mission

Upon returning to India, Vivekananda founded the Ramakrishna Math and the Ramakrishna Mission in 1897. These organizations focused on spiritual development, education, and social reform, emphasizing service to humanity as a means of serving the Divine.

 

Philosophical Contributions

Vivekananda was a proponent of Advaita Vedanta, advocating for the idea of the unity of all existence. He emphasized self-realization, the potential divinity of individuals, and the importance of serving others.

 

Literary Works

His notable works include "Raja Yoga," "Karma Yoga," "Bhakti Yoga," and "Jnana Yoga," which are compilations of his lectures and writings on various paths of spirituality.

 

Legacy

Swami Vivekananda passed away on July 4, 1902, at Belur Math near Calcutta. His teachings continue to inspire millions worldwide, promoting interfaith understanding, spiritual growth, and social service. In honor of his contributions, his birthday is celebrated as National Youth Day in India.
